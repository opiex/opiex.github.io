Finance App

Yo. I tried to take inspiration from the clean design in the apps that you mentioned. I created a few design variations, and I worked mostly on the main screens and alerts (I suggest calling them 'Alert Settings'), which are two very important aspecsts of the app.

What changed

Since the hamburger menu won't have very significant actions for now, I replaced it with three dots to make it feel less important the users.

Also - I moved the filters to have its own button instead of being part of the scrollable cards. I believe it's a task that shouldn't happen too often (as opposed to reading your transactions), so it shouldn't add friction for users who simply want to see their balance and transactions.

Alert Settings

For the alerts I really liked the toggles and scrollbars you suggested.

I did add one line of description text when a toggle is 'on' to make the settings even more understandable for less sophisticated users.

To help the users stay in context, I used a drawer element which is pretty common in apps, closing it is done by pressing the grey area.

I assumed onboarding needs to take this feature into account. This is what I was thinking:
Once a user onboards, they get to the main page. After 1 second, this automatically opens (we want to encourage them to use it). They close it, and they see that 3rd screenshot that hints the location of the icon, to help them during the next time they'll want to edit it.
